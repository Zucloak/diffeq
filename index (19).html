1. Definition, Classification, Order, Degree, and Linearity of Differential Equations

Definition

A differential equation is an equation that contains one or more derivatives of one or more unknown functions (dependent variables) with respect to one or more independent variables.

Classification by Type

  • Ordinary Differential Equation (ODE): Contains only ordinary derivatives
  • Partial Differential Equation (PDE): Contains partial derivatives

Examples

ODE: \( \frac{dy}{dx} + 2xy = e^{-x^2} \)

PDE: \( \frac{\partial^2 u}{\partial x^2} + \frac{\partial^2 u}{\partial y^2} = 0 \) (Laplace's Equation)

Order and Degree

  • Order: The order of the highest derivative in the equation
  • Degree: The exponent of the highest ordered derivative when the equation is expressed as a polynomial in derivatives
Equation Order Degree
\( \frac{d^2y}{dx^2} + 3\frac{dy}{dx} + 2y = 0 \) 2 1
\( \left(\frac{d^3y}{dx^3}\right)^2 + \left(\frac{dy}{dx}\right)^4 = 1 \) 3 2

Linearity

A differential equation is linear if:

  • The dependent variable and all its derivatives appear to the first power only
  • No products of the dependent variable and/or its derivatives appear
  • No transcendental functions of the dependent variable and/or its derivatives appear

2. Elimination of Arbitrary Constants

The process of finding a differential equation whose general solution is a given family of curves with one or more arbitrary constants.

Procedure

  1. Write the equation of the family of curves
  2. Differentiate as many times as the number of arbitrary constants
  3. Eliminate the arbitrary constants from the original equation and its derivatives

Example

Eliminate the arbitrary constant from \( y = ce^{2x} \)

Solution:

Differentiate: \( \frac{dy}{dx} = 2ce^{2x} \)

From the original equation: \( c = ye^{-2x} \)

Substitute: \( \frac{dy}{dx} = 2ye^{-2x}e^{2x} = 2y \)

Differential equation: \( \frac{dy}{dx} - 2y = 0 \)

3. Family of Curves

A family of curves is a set of curves represented by an equation containing one or more arbitrary constants.

Example

Find the differential equation for all circles with radius 1.

Solution:

Equation: \( (x-a)^2 + (y-b)^2 = 1 \)

Differentiate twice and eliminate a and b to get the differential equation.

4. Variable Separable Equations

A first-order differential equation is separable if it can be written in the form:

\[ h(y)dy = g(x)dx \]

Solution Method

\[ \int h(y)dy = \int g(x)dx + C \]

Example

Solve \( \frac{dy}{dx} = \frac{x}{y} \)

Solution:

\( ydy = xdx \)

\( \int ydy = \int xdx \)

\( \frac{y^2}{2} = \frac{x^2}{2} + C \)

\( y^2 = x^2 + K \) (where \( K = 2C \))

5. Homogeneous Differential Equations

A function \( f(x,y) \) is homogeneous of degree n if \( f(tx,ty) = t^n f(x,y) \) for all \( t > 0 \).

A differential equation \( M(x,y)dx + N(x,y)dy = 0 \) is homogeneous if both M and N are homogeneous functions of the same degree.

Solution Method

Use substitution \( y = vx \) or \( x = uy \) to transform into a separable equation.

Example

Solve \( (x^2 + y^2)dx + 2xydy = 0 \)

Solution:

Substitute \( y = vx \), \( dy = vdx + xdv \)

This transforms the equation to a separable form that can be integrated.

6. Exact Differential Equations

The DE \( M(x,y)dx + N(x,y)dy = 0 \) is exact if:

\[ \frac{\partial M}{\partial y} = \frac{\partial N}{\partial x} \]

Then there exists a function \( f(x,y) \) such that \( df = Mdx + Ndy \), and the solution is \( f(x,y) = C \).

Example

Solve \( (2xy + y^2)dx + (x^2 + 2xy)dy = 0 \)

Solution:

Check exactness: \( \frac{\partial M}{\partial y} = 2x + 2y = \frac{\partial N}{\partial x} \)

Find f such that \( \frac{\partial f}{\partial x} = M \) and \( \frac{\partial f}{\partial y} = N \)

Solution: \( x^2y + xy^2 = C \)

7. Non-Exact Differential Equations

If \( M(x,y)dx + N(x,y)dy = 0 \) is not exact, we can sometimes find an integrating factor \( \mu(x,y) \) such that:

\[ \mu M dx + \mu N dy = 0 \]

is exact.

Finding Integrating Factors

  • If \( \frac{M_y - N_x}{N} \) is a function of x only, then \( \mu(x) = e^{\int \frac{M_y - N_x}{N} dx} \)
  • If \( \frac{N_x - M_y}{M} \) is a function of y only, then \( \mu(y) = e^{\int \frac{N_x - M_y}{M} dy} \)

8. First Order Linear Differential Equations

A first-order linear differential equation has the form:

\[ \frac{dy}{dx} + P(x)y = Q(x) \]

Solution Method

Use integrating factor \( \mu(x) = e^{\int P(x)dx} \)

\[ y = \frac{1}{\mu(x)} \int \mu(x)Q(x)dx \]

9. Bernoulli's Equations

A Bernoulli equation has the form:

\[ \frac{dy}{dx} + P(x)y = Q(x)y^n \]

Solution Method

Substitute \( v = y^{1-n} \) to transform into a linear equation.

Example

Solve \( \frac{dy}{dx} + xy = xy^2 \)

Solution:

Substitute \( v = y^{-1} \), then \( \frac{dv}{dx} - xv = -x \)

This is now a linear equation that can be solved using an integrating factor.

10. Orthogonal Trajectories

Orthogonal trajectories are curves that intersect each curve of a given family at right angles.

Finding Orthogonal Trajectories

  1. Find the differential equation of the given family
  2. Replace \( \frac{dy}{dx} \) with \( -\frac{dx}{dy} \)
  3. Solve the new differential equation

Example

Find orthogonal trajectories of \( x^2 + y^2 = c^2 \)

Solution:

Differential equation: \( x + y\frac{dy}{dx} = 0 \)

For orthogonal trajectories: \( x - y\frac{dx}{dy} = 0 \)

Solution: \( y = kx \)

11. Growth and Decay

Exponential Growth/Decay

\[ \frac{dy}{dt} = ky \quad \Rightarrow \quad y = y_0 e^{kt} \]

Logistic Growth (Verhulst's Model)

\[ \frac{dP}{dt} = rP\left(1 - \frac{P}{K}\right) \]

where P is population, r is growth rate, and K is carrying capacity.

Example: Population Growth

A population doubles in 5 years. How long to triple?

Solution:

\( P(t) = P_0 2^{t/5} \), solve \( 3 = 2^{t/5} \) for t

\( t = \frac{5 \ln 3}{\ln 2} \approx 7.92 \) years

12. Newton's Law of Cooling

\[ \frac{dT}{dt} = -k(T - T_m), \quad k > 0 \]

where T is temperature of the body, T_m is ambient temperature.

Example

13. Investment Problems

Continuously Compounded Interest

\[ y = y_0 e^{kt} \]

where y_0 is initial investment, k is interest rate, and t is time.

Example

$20,000 invested at 5% compounded continuously. Find amount after 3 years.

Solution: \( y = 20000e^{0.05 \times 3} \approx $23,236.68 \)

14. Mixture Problems

\[ \frac{dS}{dt} = r_{in}S_{in} - r_{out}S_{out} \]

where S(t) is amount of substance at time t, r_in and r_out are flow rates, S_in and S_out are concentrations.

Example

A tank contains 200L of fluid with 30g of salt. Brine with 1g/L salt is pumped in at 4L/min; well-mixed solution pumped out at same rate. Find salt amount at time t.

15. Newton's Second Law of Motion

\[ m\frac{dv}{dt} = mg - \gamma v \]

where m is mass, g is gravity, γ is air resistance coefficient, and v is velocity.

Example

A body of mass 5 slugs dropped from 100 ft with zero velocity. No air resistance. Find velocity and position at any time t, and time to reach ground.

16. Simple Circuit Problems

RL Circuit

\[ L\frac{dI}{dt} + RI = E \]

RC Circuit

\[ \frac{dq}{dt} + \frac{1}{RC} q = \frac{E}{R} \]

Example: RL Circuit

An RL circuit has E=5V, R=50Ω, L=1H, no initial current. Find current at any time t.

Solution: Solve \( \frac{dI}{dt} + 50I = 5 \) with I(0)=0.

17. Variation of Parameters

Method for Second Order LDEs

For \( y'' + p_1(x)y' + p_0y = q(x) \) with complementary solution \( y_c = c_1y_1 + c_2y_2 \):

\[ y_p(x) = -y_1 \int \frac{y_2(x)q(x)}{W(y_1, y_2)(x)} dx + y_2 \int \frac{y_1(x)q(x)}{W(y_1, y_2)} dx \]

where W is the Wronskian: \( W(y_1, y_2) = y_1y_2' - y_2y_1' \)

18. Homogeneous Linear Differential Equations with Constant Coefficients

An nth-order linear differential equation with constant coefficients has the form:

\[ a_ny^{(n)} + a_{n-1}y^{(n-1)} + ... + a_1y' + a_0y = 0 \]

Solutions Based on Roots of Characteristic Equation

For \( ay'' + by' + cy = 0 \), the characteristic equation is \( a\lambda^2 + b\lambda + c = 0 \).

Case Roots General Solution
1 Real and distinct: \( \lambda_1, \lambda_2 \) \( y = c_1 e^{\lambda_1 x} + c_2 e^{\lambda_2 x} \)
2 Real and equal: \( \lambda_0 \) \( y = c_1 e^{\lambda_0 x} + c_2 x e^{\lambda_0 x} \)
3 Complex conjugates: \( \lambda_0 \pm i\theta \) \( y = e^{\lambda_0 x}(c_1 \cos \theta x + c_2 \sin \theta x) \)

Example

Find general solution to \( y'' + 9y' + 8y = 0 \)

Solution:

Characteristic equation: \( \lambda^2 + 9\lambda + 8 = 0 \)

Roots: \( \lambda = -1, -8 \)

General solution: \( y = c_1e^{-x} + c_2e^{-8x} \)

  • For complex roots: Results will show as complex numbers
  • For higher degree equations: Use MODE → EQN → 3 or 4

19. Laplace Transforms

Definition

The Laplace transform of \( f(x) \), denoted \( L\{f(x)\} \) or \( F(s) \), is:

\[ L\{f(x)\} = F(s) = \int_{0}^{\infty} e^{-sx}f(x)dx \]

for all values of s for which the improper integral converges.

Linearity Property

\[ L\{c_1f(x) + c_2g(x)\} = c_1L\{f(x)\} + c_2L\{g(x)\} \]

Common Laplace Transforms

Function Laplace Transform
\( 1 \) \( \frac{1}{s} \)
\( x \) \( \frac{1}{s^2} \)
\( x^n \) \( \frac{n!}{s^{n+1}} \)
\( e^{\alpha x} \) \( \frac{1}{s - \alpha} \)
\( \sin(\alpha x) \) \( \frac{\alpha}{s^2 + \alpha^2} \)
\( \cos(\alpha x) \) \( \frac{s}{s^2 + \alpha^2} \)
